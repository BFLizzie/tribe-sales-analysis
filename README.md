# Tribe Sales Analysis
## Description
This Excel based sales analysis project examines Tribe sales performance across locations, time periods, and product categories to generate insights that support revenue growth and market strategy decisions.

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analysing sales transaction data to evaluate performance across regions, markets, and stores. The dataset captures sales activity over time and provides insights into revenue generation, units sold, and business trends across different product models and lines of business.

## Data Content
#### 30,800 rows in Total
#### 10 columns in Total

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel (Functions, Pivot Tables, Charts, Dashboard Design)

## Data Cleaning Actions
Several data cleaning steps were applied to ensure data accuracy, consistency and reliability before analysis.

### Errors and Inconsistencies Addressed
- Standardized text fields such as **Region**, **Market**, and **Store** to ensure consistent naming
- Corrected inconsistent date formats in **Trade Date** and **Fiscal Period**
- Removed duplicate records where applicable
- Validated numeric fields (**Revenue** and **Units Sold**) to ensure correct data types
- Handled missing or blank values appropriately

## Findings
- The North East region records the highest units sold and revenue, making it the strongest performing region.
- Units sold show a strong positive relationship with revenue, indicating volume driven sales performance.
- Service Plans generate the highest revenue among the lines of business, while Parts record the highest units sold.
- A small number of stores contribute a significant share of total regional revenue, with several stores underperforming.
- Revenue is concentrated in a few major markets, while many markets show moderate but consistent performance.
- Ekiti emerges as the highest revenue generating market.

## Conclusion
Sales performance is driven by regions and markets, with the North East leading in both units sold and revenue. The strong link between units sold and revenue confirms a volume driven sales model. Service Plans contribute the most revenue, while Parts lead in sales volume, and performance is concentrated in a few high-performing stores and markets such as Ekiti.
